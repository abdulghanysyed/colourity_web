12-Season vs 4-Season Colour Analysis: What's the Difference?

Quick answer: The 4-season system groups people into Spring, Summer, Autumn, or Winter based on undertone and general brightness. The 12-season system takes those same four groups and splits each one further by depth and clarity, giving a more precise (but more complex) result. Both are built on the same underlying colour theory — 12-season is simply a more detailed version of 4-season, not a competing idea.

Where Both Systems Come From

Seasonal colour analysis started as a 4-season model in the early 1980s, grouping people by undertone and brightness into the four seasonal names. As the method grew more popular with professional stylists, practitioners found that some people didn't fit neatly into just four boxes — leading to the 12-season expansion, which adds depth and clarity as extra dimensions within each season.

The Comparison

4-Season 12-Season
Basis Undertone + general brightness Undertone + depth + clarity
Precision Broad, easier starting point More precise, fewer "close but not quite" matches
Best for Quick, general shopping guidance Fine-tuning a wardrobe, building a full palette
Complexity Simple to understand and remember Takes more explanation, easy to overthink

Which One Should You Actually Use?

For everyday shopping decisions, the 4-season basics (your undertone plus roughly how bright or muted you should go) already get you 80% of the way to good choices. The 12-season refinement mostly matters when you're building out a full, precise wardrobe or when your traits sit right on the edge between two groups.

Choose the 4-Season System if:

You want quick, easy rules and are a casual shopper looking for a starting baseline.

Choose the 12-Season System if:

You have borderline skin traits, are building a complete capsule wardrobe, or want advanced styling advice.

Do You Need to Know Your Exact Season Name?

Not really — what actually changes your shopping and styling decisions is knowing your undertone, depth, and clarity in plain terms. The specific season label (whether it's "Warm Autumn" in the 12-season system or just "Autumn" in the 4-season one) is a convenient shorthand, not something you need memorised to dress well.

FAQ

Which system is more accurate?

Neither is "more correct" — 12-season is more granular, which helps with borderline cases, but both are built on the same core undertone and contrast principles.

Can I get good results using just the 4-season basics?

Yes, for most everyday shopping. The extra precision of 12-season mainly helps when you're right on the edge between two groups or building a very deliberate, complete wardrobe.
